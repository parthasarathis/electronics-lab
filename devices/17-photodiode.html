Teaching Points
· Reverse-biased P-N junction: zero applied current in dark
· Photons create electron-hole pairs → photocurrent ∝ irradiance
· Responsivity R = η·(e/hν) depends on quantum efficiency & wavelength
· Higher reverse bias → wider depletion → faster response, lower capacitance
· NEP (noise-equivalent power) = dark current noise / responsivity
· Applications: imaging sensors, light detectors, optocouplers, rangefinders

Photodiode Physics
· Photoelectric effect: hν > Eg
· Quantum efficiency η typically 50-80%
· Peak λ near bandgap (Si: 950 nm)
· Lower capacitance with reverse bias
